## 🎯 Primary Persona: Sarah Chen

### Demographics
- **Age:** 32
- **Location:** San Francisco / Hong Kong
- **Occupation:** Product Manager at a tech startup
- **Income:** $120,000 - $150,000 USD
- **Education:** MBA

### Psychographics
- **Values:** Mindfulness, self-improvement, work-life balance
- **Mindset:** "Life is short, I want to make it count"
- **Pain Points:** 
  - Feels time slipping away without noticing
  - Struggles to maintain daily reflection practice
  - Wants to build better habits but needs motivation
- **Motivations:**
  - Living intentionally
  - Leaving a positive legacy
  - Making every week matter

### Tech Profile
- **Devices:** iPhone 15 Pro, MacBook Pro, Apple Watch
- **Apps Used Daily:**
  - Calm (meditation)
  - Notion (organization)
  - Streaks (habit tracking)
  - Headspace (sleep)
  - Instagram (social, ~15 min/day)
- **Tech Savvy:** High
- **Design Preferences:** Clean, minimal, warm aesthetics

### Behavior Patterns
- **Morning Routine:**
  7:00 AM - Wake up, check phone
  7:15 AM - 10-min meditation (Calm)
  7:30 AM - Review daily tasks (Notion)
  
- **Evening Routine:**
  10:00 PM - Reflect on day
  10:15 PM - Light reading
  10:30 PM - Sleep

- **Weekly Reflection:**
  Sunday evening - Review week, plan next week

### Needs & Goals
**Primary Needs:**
1. **Visual reminder of life's finiteness** → LifeGrid
2. **Daily accountability** → Habit tracker
3. **Gratitude practice** → Journal feature
4. **Motivation to live intentionally** → The Clock

**Goals with Mori:**
- Check LifeGrid daily as morning reminder
- Log habit (+/-) every evening
- Write 3 gratitude entries before bed
- Feel more "present" and less anxious about time

### Pain Points with Current Solutions
- **Streaks:** Gamified, feels superficial
- **Daylio:** Too many features, overwhelming
- **Paper journal:** Easy to forget, no reminders
- **We Croak:** Too stark, needs more warmth

### Ideal Mori Experience
```
Morning: Open Mori, see LifeGrid, feel motivated
Evening: Quick habit check-in, 2-minute gratitude
Weekly: Review week, feel sense of progress
```

### Quote
> "I want an app that reminds me life is precious, not in a depressing way, but in a way that makes me want to live better."

## 🎨 Design Implications by Persona

### For Sarah (Primary)
- **Onboarding:** Quick, 30-second intro to LifeGrid
- **Tone:** Warm, encouraging, not preachy
- **Notifications:** Gentle morning/evening reminders
- **Copy:** "Make today count" (not "You will die")

### For Marcus (Secondary)
- **Settings:** Minimalist mode (hide extra features)
- **Tone:** Intellectual, respectful, deep
- **Copy:** "Weekly reflection" (not "Daily win streak")
- **Feature:** Export journal to PDF (for teaching)

### For Emma (Tertiary)
- **Aesthetics:** Premium, warm minimalism essential
- **Feature:** Screenshot-friendly LifeGrid (for Instagram)
- **Customization:** Font options, color themes
- **Social:** Optional sharing (not forced)

### For David (Edge Case)
- **Accessibility:** Dynamic Type, VoiceOver support
- **Simplification:** "Simple Mode" toggle
- **Export:** Data portability (JSON/PDF)
- **Legacy:** "Life review" mode (past-focused)

## 📝 User Interview Questions

### For Sarah (Target User)
1. How do you currently track your habits and reflect on life?
2. What apps do you use daily, and why do you love them?
3. How do you feel when you see time passing (e.g., birthdays)?
4. What would make you open a life-tracking app every day?
5. What's your biggest fear about time/life?

### For Marcus (Philosophical User)
1. How do you currently practice Memento Mori?
2. What's missing from existing philosophical tools?
3. How can an app respect the gravity of mortality?
4. What features would feel authentic vs. commercial?
5. How do you share wisdom with students/others?

### For Emma (Aesthetic User)
1. What makes an app feel "beautiful" to you?
2. Which apps do you admire for their design?
3. Would you share LifeGrid screenshots on social media?
4. What design elements feel warm vs. cold?
5. How important is customization to you?
